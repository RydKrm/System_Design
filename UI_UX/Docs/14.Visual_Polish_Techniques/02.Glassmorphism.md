### Glassmorphism вҖ” The Aesthetics of Frosted Glass

Glassmorphism is a visual design style that simulates the appearance of frosted glass вҖ” a translucent material through which background content can be seen, softened by a blur effect that obscures detail while allowing light and color to pass through. The style emerged as a dominant trend in digital design around 2020вҖ“2021, driven in part by Apple's adoption of frosted glass as the primary surface treatment in macOS Big Sur and iOS, and popularized through a widely shared design specification by the designer Michal Malewicz.

The essential components of a glassmorphic surface are four: a **background blur** that softens the content visible behind the element, a **semi-transparent background color** (most commonly white or a very light tint of the surrounding colors, with low opacity), a **subtle border** on the top and left edges of the element that simulates the reflective edge of real glass, and a gentle **drop shadow** to anchor the element spatially. The effect works most powerfully when the background behind the glass element is a rich, colorful gradient or a vibrant photograph вҖ” because the blur transforms those colors into a soft, shifting chromatic backdrop that is visible through the glass, giving the surface its characteristic luminous quality.
