## Segmentation — Why Aggregate Data Lies

### The Tyranny of the Average User

One of the most consequential analytical errors in product design is the interpretation of aggregate data as if it described a single, representative user — as if there were an "average user" whose behavior the analytics data portrays. In reality, aggregate data is almost always a blend of behaviors from multiple distinct user populations, and the averaged blend may accurately describe none of them.

The classic illustration is the statistician who drowned in a river with an average depth of six inches. The average was accurate — in aggregate, the river was shallow. But specific sections were twenty feet deep, and those sections killed him. Aggregate conversion rates, retention curves, and engagement metrics work the same way: a product with a 5% overall conversion rate may have a 25% conversion rate from one traffic source and 0.5% from another. Designing for "5% conversion" is designing for a fiction.
