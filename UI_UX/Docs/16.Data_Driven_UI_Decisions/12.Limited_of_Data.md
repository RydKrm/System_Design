## The Limits of Data — What Numbers Can Never Tell You

### The Most Important Chapter

This guide has spent twelve chapters explaining, with considerable enthusiasm, the power and precision of data-driven UI decisions. This final chapter exists to argue the opposite case — not to negate what came before, but to complete it. Because the most dangerous practitioner in product design is not the designer who ignores data. It is the designer who believes that data is sufficient — that everything important about human experience with a product is measurable, and that unmeasured things do not exist or do not matter.

Data can measure what people do. It cannot measure why, except by inference. It can measure how many users abandoned a form. It cannot measure how frustrated or violated they felt while doing so. It can measure the click-through rate of two versions of a headline. It cannot measure which version of the headline accurately represented the product and which slightly overpromised it. It can measure engagement. It cannot measure whether that engagement was joyful or compulsive, healthy or regretted. It can measure conversion. It cannot measure whether the converting users genuinely needed the product or were persuaded into a purchase they would come to regret.
