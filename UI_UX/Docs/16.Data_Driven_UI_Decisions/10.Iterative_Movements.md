## Iterative Improvement — The Full Design Loop

### The Anatomy of a Learning Cycle

Iterative improvement is the practice of continuously improving a product through repeated cycles of observation, hypothesis, design, implementation, and measurement. It is the structural backbone of data-driven design — the process that converts raw data into improved user experiences over time. Without a disciplined iterative process, even the most sophisticated data collection produces no improvement: teams gather insights, discuss them enthusiastically, produce redesigns that are never shipped, and eventually stop collecting the data because it does not seem to lead anywhere.

The iterative loop has a specific structure that must be honored for it to function. Skipping steps, compressing them, or reordering them consistently produces worse outcomes than following the full loop even when the loop feels slow. The reason is that each step performs a distinct function that the other steps cannot substitute for: observation without hypothesis leads to analysis paralysis; hypothesis without design expertise leads to weak interventions; design without implementation leads to portfolio documents that never change user behavior; implementation without measurement produces effort that cannot be learned from.

### The Documentation Discipline — Building Institutional Memory

The single most undervalued practice in iterative improvement is systematic documentation of both wins and losses. Teams that document only their successful experiments lose half the value of their testing program. Failed experiments are not failures — they are evidence. They tell you which of your intuitions about user behavior were wrong, which approaches don't work in your specific context, and which hypotheses have already been tested so they don't need to be tested again. A team that has documented fifty failed experiments has a fifty-item list of things that don't work for their users — an enormously valuable asset that a new team member, or a new designer joining the team, cannot access without that documentation.
