## 🧠 1. Foundations: How Humans Think

### 1️⃣ **Thinking, Fast and Slow – by Daniel Kahneman**

This is _foundational_.  
It explains:

- System 1 vs System 2 thinking
    
- Cognitive biases
    
- Decision shortcuts
    

👉 As a web developer, this helps you design:

- Better CTAs
    
- Better onboarding flows
    
- Smarter pricing pages
    

This book will change how you see user behavior.

---

### 2️⃣ **Predictably Irrational – by Dan Ariely**

Focus: Why users don’t behave logically.

You’ll understand:

- Why “Free” is powerful
    
- Why pricing tiers work
    
- Why comparison changes decisions
    

Perfect for product strategy thinking.

---

## 🎯 2. Psychology for Product & UX

### 3️⃣ **Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products – by Nir Eyal**

This is very practical.

Teaches:

- Trigger → Action → Variable reward → Investment loop
    
- How products like social apps create habits
    

If you're building scalable systems (like your social feed or AI app idea), this book is directly useful.

---

### 4️⃣ **Don't Make Me Think – by Steve Krug**

Simple. Practical. Legendary.

You’ll learn:

- Users don’t read, they scan
    
- Confusion kills conversion
    
- Clarity beats cleverness
    

Every frontend developer should read this.

---

### 5️⃣ **The Design of Everyday Things – by Don Norman**

This teaches:

- Affordances
    
- Feedback
    
- Mental models
    
- Why users blame themselves for bad design
    

This one builds deep product intuition.

---

## 🔥 3. Persuasion & Behavioral Influence

### 6️⃣ **Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion – by Robert Cialdini**

Classic principles:

- Social proof
    
- Scarcity
    
- Authority
    
- Reciprocity
    

Every SaaS landing page uses this stuff.

---

### 7️⃣ **Atomic Habits – by James Clear**

Not directly product, but teaches:

- Habit formation
    
- Behavior design
    
- Small improvements philosophy
    

As a developer, this improves both your users’ habits and your own growth.

---

## 🧩 4. Advanced: Product Thinking & Strategy

### 8️⃣ **Inspired – by Marty Cagan**

More product management than psychology — but helps you think:

- What makes products succeed
    
- How product teams think
    
- User-centric product building
    

---
